Damn things

I’m not someone who uses pesticides, so I’m going to investigate in alternatives. My first thoughts are soapy water and maybe some sort of jasmonic acid/ silicon based help too to boost the plant defences.

Things are growing slowly but surely in the raised beds too - broadbeans are beginning to flower

But you can also see something has been having a good old chomp at the leave. I’ve tied them to the poles now, so hoping this helps. May put oil near the bottom sof the poles to see if this hinders their climbing skills….pesky slugs.

My Bulgarian giant leeks are also growing - but I have been growing them since LAST SUMMER! Not sure I quite have this crop in the bag just yet. Round two this summer. I’m also planning on doing some winter crops for the first time, usually I just let things go awray in winter, but this time I’m going to try for brussel sprouts, cabbages and leeks.

I’ve also sown more peas and radishes again. I’m growing mixed radishes, trying the ‘long scarlet’, ‘winter white’ and ‘mooli’ ones as well as the usual ones. The mixed beetroot and swiss chard are also starting to make tracks now the sun is shining.

Many, many aphids, slugs and caterpillars are on the lurch. The jostaberry still hasn’t got flowers, but I’ve read it should be July time before it will. Trouble is, the caterpillars can’t resist the lush green leaves on it - they also decimated the redcurrant bush beside it too

The pears are beginning to form - very pleased with this. The plum crop is going to be pretty big too. Yum! The kiwi situation isn’t great. The whitefly is pestering these plants so much. Going to have to get some silicon and sprays on the go. I’ve put a big trellis for them to climb up, so hopefully this should support the three of them.

The loganberry is growing like crazy, need to put it into the ground, but think I’ve left it too late now. Golden raspberry is growing, but I think the logan is outcompeting. Gooseberries and redcurrants at the front of the house have a huge crop increased compared to last year.

I’ve put the corn seedlings outside now, so they are in the wilderness. Last year I tried to grow ‘Fiesta’ corn. It turns out it is a field variety and grows around 1.5 - 2 m tall! Anyhoo, it wasn’t sunny enough in the raised beds and the season for it is too long so I got a few immature cobs. Boo. This year I’ve settled with my vanilla ‘swift’ and I’m happy with this choice.

Aphids.

They are destructing the life out of my artichoke plants. I had visions of me happily munching on the petals in the summer, a little oil dribbling down my chin as I enjoyed the fruits of my labour. Then came along some small farmers with a rather vicious sting. Ants.

Cue distruction! I’ve had to chop one to the ground from last year, which was barely surviving thanks to these weasles last year. After this photo I poke them with a stick and they were not happy. So many types of aphids. Damn them and their telescoping regeneration. At least they are not near the pear tree! Last year they were the cause of its poor growth. Must bug lady bird larvae for biological control.

The pink blueberry tree is looking quite sad - I think it’s the small pot it is in. But there are still flower buds so I’m hopeful it will kick back and enjoy the rays. The nectarine tree has lots of mini nectarines, so hopfully one may make it to the end this year. We’ll see.

Things are looking good in the greenhouse. The courgettes are getting bigger and I will be able to move them outside in a couple of weeks. I’m still too scared of slugs eating them at this stage.

Most things inside are looking close to being plant outside. Can’t wait to get them done and get sowing more seeds. I’m presaoking “eight ball” courgette seeds and will start them off inside this week. They produce lovely round courgettes, I love them. I’ve also been and bought more seeds (yes, I have a problem). I’m going to try and grow salsify (white roots) and scorzonera (black roots) both of which are root vegetables. You can’t eat the skin, but they are meant to taste great. It does say you need sun, so maybe not going to get the biggest crop. Looking forward to trying though. I’ve alos got some orange thyme and some summer savory for the herb patch.

Speaking of herbs, the lemon balm I bought has been transplanted and is taking very well. I also planted out some sunflowers I had germinated. Slugs have enjoyed them lots, but not the lemon balm. Interesting…

Also - FINALLY - garlic chive success! My new seeds worked. I have lost the packet thought. Need to order more, but I’m super chuffed with them! The fig tree has a couple of figs. At first I thought they were leaf buds, then I remembered the waspy hole (ostiole) and the flowers were inside out. Amazing. The walnut tree is growing really well too - it’s going to get pretty big!

To do next: sow the new seeds, succeed at growing leeks and world domination. Easy.
